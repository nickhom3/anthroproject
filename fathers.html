What is life history?




At the macro level, life history acts on a species level, generally describing the changes an organism undergoes throughout its lifespan. Humans, for example, possess a biologically unique life history, in that we experience an extended period of dependency in youth, endure relatively long lives, undergo puberty and menopause later in life, and typically look to paternal resource gathering during childhood. Life history theory studies how key events in an someones's life relate to reproductivity and mortality. Most people go through specific stages of the reproductive cycle around specific ages, but variation exists in the intra- and inter-cultural levels, and also within individuals.
